LEONARDO

INTERIZUVNI DONAJPUH
One day the smoke came out of the mighty ZX Spectrum, and the games from tape-recorder were no more.
I had to learn to program my games if I wanted to play. I would spend Hours typing the lines of code and running trial-error-correct-repeat loop between the manual on BASIC, my notebook, and my father's TV. The keyboard becomes so hot from the processor that it feels like it will burst in flames. The parents couldn't stand seeing me in front of the TV screen that long but hours were not enough. I was racing against the grownups, the burning plastics, and my little brain.
I would write down on paper everything I came up in the code, so that the next day when the ZX and parents cool down, I can come faster to where I left off.
On the 'seventh' day, one pixel appeared, changed color, moved right, and I was hooked. Ever since then, somewhere in the back of my head I was thinking about a solution to a problem I couldn't explain to anyone around me.
I was making a drawing software; I called it "Leonardo." ZX have not had a mouse or any other input device but the keyboard. I came up with a cross that would be controlled by WASD keys. I am still very proud of how sometimes that thing would allow me to draw lines for a few minutes before they shut me down.
Now, thirty-five years late(r) I am equipped with a variety of input devices, beautiful machines with touchscreen displays that float in the air, I am in LA, safe and warm, and I will make this software again, for the modern artist.
This is the first article of the journal of the development and thoughts during the process. The random images will be the first to get polished as I am working on this blog template too.
